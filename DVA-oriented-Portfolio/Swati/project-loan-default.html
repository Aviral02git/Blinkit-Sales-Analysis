What The Project Talks About

This project studies borrower and loan application data to help banks understand which applicants may be likely to default. The analysis considers income, gender, loan purpose, region, credit score, debt-to-income ratio, loan-to-value ratio, interest rate, property value, loan term, and other deterministic risk factors.

The dataset contains missing values and multicollinearity concerns, so the work focuses on cleaning, standardization, imputation, feature preparation, and dashboard-ready summaries that support risk mitigation and business decision-making.

My Contribution

I worked on transforming the raw loan data into a usable analytics asset by cleaning inconsistent categories, handling missing values, standardizing columns, preparing pivot-table calculations, and converting the results into dashboard insights and recommendations for lending strategy.

Project Workflow

1. Dataset Understanding

Reviewed borrower, credit, financial, collateral, application, and target default fields.

2. Cleaning & Preparation

Standardized loan limits, credit worthiness, loan purpose, gender, ratios, income, and application fields.

3. Risk Analysis

Compared loan amount exposure, risk distribution, credit score ranges, age groups, occupancy, and regional LTV patterns.

4. Dashboarding

Built dashboard summaries for default probability, risk segmentation, demographic analysis, and financial KPIs.

Key Insights

  • The total loan amount analyzed is approximately $6.6 billion.
  • High-risk loans account for 2,007 loans and around $675 million in exposure.
  • The 35-44 age group commands the highest total loan amount at about $1.67 billion.
  • The Central region has the highest average loan-to-value ratio at 76.69%.
  • Interest rates remain unusually stable across credit score ranges, suggesting the need for better risk-based pricing.

Recommendations

  • Apply stricter approval rules or additional buffers for high-risk borrower segments.
  • Review pricing strategy so better credit profiles receive more appropriate interest rates.
  • Use regional LTV differences to adjust collateral review and lending limits.
  • Tailor loan products and marketing for the dominant professional borrower segment.
